Signal Classification

Continuous Time and Discrete Time

Continuous (CT): Analog, defined in all instants of time s(t)s(t)s(t)

Discrete (DT): a continuous signal sampled to intervals of time s(n⋅TS)s(n \cdot T_S)s(n⋅TS​)

Periodic and Aperiodic

Periodic: repeated after time TO(CT)T_O (CT)TO​(CT), or number of samples NNN (e.g. sine, square, sawtooth)

Formulae:

  • continuous signals: x(t)=x(t+TO)x(t) = x(t + T_O)x(t)=x(t+TO​)
  • discrete signals: x(n)=x(n+N)x(n) = x(n + N)x(n)=x(n+N)

Aperiodic: a signal exhibiting no repetition
